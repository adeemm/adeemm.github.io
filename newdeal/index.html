About Us

What We Do

We work tirelessly to make sure the Great Depression will never happen again, by attacking one of its root causes.

Why We Do It

Stock Market speculation and irresponsible investing/spending had a horrific effect on the American people and we need to make sure this never happens again.

How We Do It

We prohibit the combination of commercial and investment banking to protect your assets and put vital safeguards in place.

Our Goal

Our goal is to stop the unprecedented run on banks and restore public confidence in the US banking system.

In order to get the United States out of this horrible depression, we must sever the harmful linkages between commercial and investment banking. These ties were a large cause of the Great Depression and will help prevent this horrendous atrocity from ever happening again.

Mission Statement

Our goal is to fix the broken banking system of the United States. We will help get the country out of this Great Depression if you invest in us.

This program will be used all over the country in America's banks and investment agencies.

Our History

  • 1929: Approximate beginning of the Great Depression
  • 1930: Early introduction of the bill
  • 1933: Bill signed into law by President Roosevelt
  • 1935: Failed attempt to repeal the act
  • 1999: Gramm–Leach–Bliley Act repeals the act
  • 2007/2008: Financial crisis (sometimes attributed to the act's repeal)
  • 2009/2010: Failed efforts to restore the act

Our Services

Ban Irresponsible Investing

Banks are becomming too greedy with your money and sloppily investing it in high-risk assets, leading to huge losses.

Create the FDIC

We will create the Federal Deposit Insurance Corporation (FDIC) to insure/protect the money that you deposit in banks.

Restore Trust in Banks

Mssive bank runs are only making the depression worse. We must work to restore public confidence in banks, so we can end this terrible depression.

Create the FOMC

We will create the Federal Open Market Committee (FOMC) to regulate and control the United States' money supply and interest rates.

Regulate Speculation

Banks must be monitored to ensure there is not undue use of credit for speculative trading. We will also limit the loans member banks can make.
