United Nations Breach

& Cover-Up

Presentation by: Adeem Mawani

Background Info

  • Occured in mid-July of 2019
  • Unauthorized access to over 400GB of data
  • At least 42 different compromised servers

The Breach

  • Previously unknown until recently, when a leaked confidential IT report surfaced
  • Not announced to the public
  • Even staff were not made aware, just asked to change passwords

The Breach Cont.

  • The breach affected dozens of servers in three separate locations:
    • UN Office at Vienna
    • UN Office at Geneva
    • UN Office of the High Commissioner for Human Rights (OHCHR) headquarters in Geneva

Leaked Report

  • Only internal IT teams and the chiefs of the UN Office at Geneva and the UN Office at Vienna were informed
  • Lateral movement across multiple AD domains
    • “ We are working under the assumption that the entire domain is compromised... ”

The Breach Cont.

  • Possible breach of GDPR? (EU General Data Protection Regulation)
  • Nope, UN has diplomatic status, so it has immunity from the legal process

Who's behind the breach?

  • Not yet attributed
  • Most likely nation-state actors
  • Some APT (Advanced Persistent Threat) group

How did they do it?

  • Used CVE-2019-0604
  • Microsoft SharePoint RCE Vulnerability
  • Patch was released in February, and the attack occured in July
