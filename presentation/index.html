Corp.com For Sale

One of the most dangerous domains

Presentation by: Adeem Mawani

Background Info

  • Early investor Mike O'Connor bought several short domain names back in 1994
  • These included bar.com, grill.com, and corp.com among others
  • Until now, he has refused to auction off corp.com because of how sensitive it is

Why Auction?

  • After purchasing these domains early on, they soon increased in value as the internet grew
  • He held on to corp.com until now
  • The asking price is 1.7 million

The Dangerous Domain

  • So why is corp.com so dangerous?
  • The root of the problem lies with early Windows versions and namespace collisions

Namespace Collision?

  • A "namespace collision" is when a domain name intended to be used only on an internal company network ends up overlapping with domains that resolve normally on the open Internet

Misconfigurations

  • Early Windows AD setup a default domain of corp.com
  • Unless otherwise configured with a proper company controlled domain

DNS Devolution

  • Allows for shorthand acess of shared drives
  • "\\drive1\" instead of "drive1.internalnetwork.example.com"

DNS Devolution Cont.

  • This causes trouble whenever a company asset leaves the internal network
  • For example, at Starbucks, the computer will try to login to the internal drive, but since corp.com also resovles on the open internet, whoever owns the domain can easily harvest credentials

Traffic Analysis

  • Security researcher Jeff Schmidt aquired funding from the Department of Homeland Security to study DNS devolution
  • During an 8 month analysis in 2019, Schmidt found more than 375,000 Windows PCs sending the site credentials

Traffic Analysis Cont.

  • When corp.com was setup to accept incoming SMTP traffic, they received 12 million emails
  • They included pre-releases of corporate financial filings with The U.S. Securities and Exchange Commission, human resources reports, and other scary things

Mitigations

  • Many companies have neglected patching because it would require taking down the entire Active Directory network for a while
