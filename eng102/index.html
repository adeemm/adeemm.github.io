Good Country People

By: Adeem Mawani, Aisha Alston, & Kaitlyn Kelly

Author Biography

  • Flannery O’Connor was born on March 25, 1925 (in Savannah, Georgia) and died August 3, 1964
  • Her father died when she was only 15 years old
  • Wrote fiction works in the Southern Gothic style
    • Grotesque elements
    • Flawed characters
    • Dark humor

Influences

  • Religion - a central theme in many of her works
    • She was born and raised in the South
    • This region was very religious (AKA the "Bible Belt")
    • Her family were one of the oldest Catholic families in Georgia
    • Later, her family moved to the religious town of Milledgeville, which inspired much of her writing

Historical Context

  • Her works were written during the Civil Rights era in the South
  • However, these conecpts were largely left out of her works

Characters

  • Hulga / Joy - Protagonist
    • Starts out as a cold, isolated character (changed her name to Hulga from Joy)
    • Vulnerable with her weak heart condition and wooden leg
    • Later begins to open up to Manley
  • Manley Pointer - Antagonist
    • Traveling Bible salesman
    • Manipulates Hulga
    • Steals her wooden leg
    • Claims that he travels around stealing from those with disabilities

Characters Cont.

  • Mrs. Hopewell
    • Hulga's mother
    • Divorced, runs the family farm by herself
    • Cares for Hulga and tries to empathize with her bad attitude
  • Mrs. Freeman
    • Tenant farmer hired by Mrs. Hopewell

Plot Summary











  1. Manley arrives to sell Bibles
  2. He lands a date with Hulga
  3. They go to a quiet place (a barn)
  4. They kiss and talk about her leg
  5. Manley reveals his true intentions
  6. He steals her wood leg and glasses






Climax: Hulga discovering Manley isn't what he seems





Conflict: Hulga vs. Manley the Bible salesman

Setting

  • The story takes place on a southern farm sometime in the mid-1950s
    • The beginning takes place in Mrs. Hopewell's kitchen, where Hulga, Mrs. Hopewell, and Mrs. Freeman all eat together
    • Later, Hulga and Manley have their date inside of a barn
    • The enclosed space in the barn repressents Hulga's vulnerability after Manley takes off her leg

Themes

  • Identity
    • Mrs. Hopewell labels people throughout the story as "good country people"
    • However, she ends up mislabeling Manley
  • Innocence
    • Hulga has a PhD and several years of education, but cannot recognize that Manley is a con man
  • Religion
    • Shows the contrast between atheism (Hulga) and religion
    • Manley exemplifies how Christianity / religion is abused

Imagery & Symbolism

  • Hulga's wooden leg
    • Symbolizes Hulga's vulnerability and identity
    • It's part of what makes her unique
    • She goes to great lengths to protect and shield it
  • Manley's suitcase
    • Contains a hollowed Bible with whiskey and condoms inside the pages
    • Shows that everything is not what it seems
    • Represents hypocrisy, sometimes the faithful are actually the worst sinners

Narrator Point of View

  • The story uses third-person limited point of view
  • The reader can only see into the minds of Hulga and Mrs. Hopewell
